abstract: "Multi-user can collaboratively complete complex visualization tasks that cannot be completed by a single user. Although the system of multi-user collaboration has made great progress, there are many challenges in the collaborative visualization of 3-D volume data due to the difficulties in task division, multi-user collaboration, and collaborative slice analysis. This paper proposes a client-server (CS)-based collaborative visualization system, which consists of a 3-D volume explorer and a 2-D slice analyzer, to help domain experts to fully utilize their background domain knowledge to illustrate different parts of the data. For example, the brain surgeon expert, pulmonologist, and cardiologist can visualize and analyze the different subsets of the volume data, i.e., the corresponding sub-volumes of the brain, heart, lungs, and blood vessels. It also allows to take full advantage of the hardware resources, because all the computation intensive tasks can be allocated to the powerful sever while the interactive tasks can be allocated to the clients. We evaluate the system by collecting the feedback from domain experts and the people who are unfamiliar with medical data visualization or analysis. The domain experts’ feedback shows that the 3-D volume explorer and the 2-D slice analyzer are capable of peer-expert discussion and medical case teaching, respectively. Furthermore, the evaluation shows the usability of the system for the people unfamiliar with the domain."
